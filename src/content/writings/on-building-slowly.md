There is a particular kind of impatience that dresses itself up as ambition. It wants the finished thing without the unfinished middle, the result without the residue of getting there. For a long time I mistook it for drive. Now I think it is mostly fear — of the slow part, where nothing looks like progress and the work refuses to be rushed.

Building slowly is not the same as building lazily. It is a decision to let a problem be exactly as complicated as it is, and no simpler. You sit with it. You make a version that is wrong in an instructive way. You throw it out. You keep the one idea from it that was worth keeping.

## The middle is the work

Most of what I have learned did not arrive at the beginning, when everything felt possible, or at the end, when it was done. It arrived in the middle, in the unglamorous stretch where the interesting decisions actually get made. The middle is where you find out what you were really building.

If I could tell my earlier self one thing, it would be this: the middle is not an obstacle between you and the work. The middle **is** the work.

## A small practice

These days I try to end each session by writing down the single most confusing thing I hit. Not to solve it — just to name it. More often than not, naming it is half the solution, and the other half is waiting for me the next morning, patient and a little smaller than it looked the day before.
